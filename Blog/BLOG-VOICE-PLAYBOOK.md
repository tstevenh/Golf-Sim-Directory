# GolfSimMap Blog Voice Playbook

Last updated: 2026-02-21

## Skill Used
- `brand-voice` (Build mode, calibrated with competitor voice patterns and current traffic/ranking benchmarks)

## Why This Exists
- Keep every article consistent in tone, structure, and reader trust.
- Borrow what works from leading golf publishers without sounding like a copycat.
- Match GolfSimMap's product role: practical decision support for indoor golf.

### Archetypes That Win
- `PGA TOUR`: The Institutional Authority (official, stats-heavy, event-first, low personality).
- `GOLF.com`: The Clubhouse Media Brand (broad coverage, punchy headlines, recurring columns/shows).
- `Golf Digest`: The Coach-Curator (teaching + gear + destinations framed as practical improvement).
- `MyGolfSpy`: The Consumer Watchdog (independent testing, anti-marketing posture, proof-driven claims).

## Recommended GolfSimMap Archetype

### Primary Archetype
- `The Trusted Caddie-Analyst`

### Secondary Flavor
- `Consumer Watchdog` (light): use proof and specifics.
- `Coach-Curator` (light): teach decisions, not swing theory.

### Positioning Statement
- GolfSimMap should sound like the smart local golfer who has already done the homework on every indoor sim option, then hands you the shortest path to the right pick.

## Voice Profile

## Voice Summary
GolfSimMap sounds practical, local, and evidence-based. We are direct without being harsh and helpful without sounding generic. Every article should make the reader feel, "I can decide faster now."

## Core Personality Traits
- `Practical`: always oriented to decision-making, not abstract golf talk.
- `Grounded`: specific details, realistic tradeoffs, no hype promises.
- `Independent`: compares options fairly and calls out weak info.
- `Local-savvy`: references real venue contexts (city, weather, commute, budget).

## Tone Spectrum
| Dimension | Position | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Formal <-> Casual | Casual-professional | Conversational, clean, no slang overload |
| Serious <-> Playful | Mostly serious with light wit | Helpful first, personality second |
| Reserved <-> Bold | Measured bold | Clear recommendations backed by evidence |
| Simple <-> Sophisticated | Simple words, structured logic | Easy scan, smart substance |
| Warm <-> Direct | Warm-direct | "Here is what to do next" tone |

## Vocabulary

### Use
- "best fit"
- "worth it if"
- "good for / not ideal for"
- "what to check before you book"
- "real-world"
- "tradeoff"

### Avoid
- "ultimate"
- "game-changing"
- "revolutionary"
- "state-of-the-art"
- "seamless"
- "elevate your experience"

### Jargon Level
- Moderate, always translated in plain English.

### Profanity
- Never.

## Rhythm and Structure
- Sentences: mostly short/medium; use short lines for key decisions.
- Paragraphs: 1-3 sentences.
- Openings: start with a real problem or decision moment.
- Formatting: clear H2s, checklists, comparison tables, "who this is for" callouts.

## POV and Reader Relationship
- First person: mostly `we` for editorial voice; occasional `I` only in first-hand testing stories.
- Reader address: direct `you`.
- Relationship stance: expert guide + local caddie.

## On-Brand vs Off-Brand

### On-Brand
- "If you care about ball data more than bar vibes, start with TrackMan-heavy venues."
- "Most golfers overpay for bay time by booking peak slots they do not need."
- "This option is great for winter reps, but not ideal for club fitting."

### Off-Brand
- "Discover the ultimate simulator experience near you." (generic hype)
- "In this comprehensive guide, we will explore..." (corporate filler)
- "Every golfer needs this setup." (overgeneralized claim)

## Editorial Do and Don't

### Do
- Lead with decision clarity in first 120 words.
- Use concrete criteria: price range, monitor type, booking friction, practice goals.
- Call out uncertainty when data is missing.
- Include quick summary boxes for skimmers.
- End with a specific next action.

### Don't
- Write like a tournament news wire.
- Use empty adjectives without proof.
- Bury the recommendation below long intros.
- Pretend all golfers have the same budget or goals.
- Copy competitor phrasing verbatim.

## Default Article Blueprint
1. Problem: "What golfers get wrong about X."
2. Fast answer: 3-5 bullet verdict.
3. Decision framework: criteria and tradeoffs.
4. Local application: how this changes by city/venue type.
5. Practical next steps: what to filter in GolfSimMap.
6. Internal CTA: link to `/search`, relevant launch monitor pages, and city pages.

## Reusable Intro Formula
- "If you're trying to [goal] this winter, the hard part is not motivation. It's picking the right indoor setup without wasting money or time. Here's how to choose in 10 minutes."
