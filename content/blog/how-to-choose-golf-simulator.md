Choosing the right golf simulator for your venue is one of the most important decisions you'll make. The technology you install will define your customers' experience and determine what kind of golfer you attract.

This guide breaks down everything you need to know — from understanding different tracking technologies to budgeting for the long term.

---

## Radar vs Camera Systems

The first major decision is choosing between radar-based and camera-based launch monitors.

### Radar Systems

Radar systems like **TrackMan** and **FlightScope** track the ball using Doppler radar positioned behind the golfer. They're excellent for:

- Full swing analysis with complete ball flight data
- Larger commercial spaces with adequate room depth
- Customers who want tour-level accuracy
- Integration with detailed coaching software

### Camera Systems

Camera-based systems like **Foresight's GCQuad** use high-speed cameras to capture impact data. They excel at:

- Working in compact spaces (less room depth required)
- Short game accuracy — chipping and putting
- Immediate feedback without waiting for ball flight
- Lower maintenance (no moving parts)

> **Pro Tip:** If your venue has limited space (under 15 feet of depth), camera systems are usually the better choice. Radar needs room to track the full ball flight.

## Budget Considerations

Entry-level systems start around $10,000, while professional-grade units can exceed $50,000. But don't just look at upfront costs — consider the total cost of ownership.

| Cost Factor | What to Expect |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Software Subscriptions** | $500–$2,000/year for course libraries and updates |
| **Maintenance** | Annual calibration, sensor cleaning, occasional repairs |
| **Upgrades** | Trade-in programs when new models release |
| **Warranty** | Most offer 1–2 years; extended warranties available |

---

## Space Requirements

Before you buy, measure your space carefully. Most simulators need:

- **Ceiling Height:** 10 feet minimum (12+ feet for comfortable driver swings)
- **Room Depth:** 15–20 feet (radar needs more than cameras)
- **Room Width:** 12–15 feet for comfortable swinging

**Camera systems** are generally more forgiving with space constraints than radar systems. If you're retrofitting an existing space, this could be your deciding factor.

---

## Software & Courses

The hardware is only half the equation. The software determines what your customers can actually *do* with your simulators.

Look for platforms that offer:

1. **Diverse Course Selection** — Famous courses, local favorites, and fictional layouts
2. **Multiple Game Modes** — Stroke play, match play, closest-to-the-pin, long drive contests
3. **Skills Challenges** — Driving ranges, approach practice, putting greens
4. **Online Play** — Compete with golfers at other venues
5. **Stat Tracking** — Let customers save their data and track improvement over time

---

## Making Your Decision

Still unsure? Here's a quick decision framework:

**Choose Radar If:**
- You have adequate space (20+ feet depth)
- Your customers are serious golfers wanting detailed data
- Budget allows for premium equipment
- You plan to offer lessons and coaching

**Choose Camera If:**
- Space is limited
- You want lower maintenance
- Short game accuracy is a priority
- You're working with a tighter budget
