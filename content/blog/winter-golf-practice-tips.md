For most golfers, winter means months of rusty swings and lost distance. But it doesn't have to. Indoor simulators offer something outdoor practice can't: **data**.

## Why Winter is Actually the Best Time to Improve

Without the pressure of keeping score or maintaining a handicap, you can focus on the parts of your game that need work. Most golfers see their biggest improvements during off-season practice.

### Key metrics to track:

- Club head speed
- Ball speed
- Launch angle
- Spin rate
- Carry distance
- Smash factor

## What to Work On

### 1. Baseline Your Numbers

First session of winter? Hit 10-15 shots with each club and record your averages. This gives you a benchmark to measure improvement against.

### 2. Pick One Thing

Don't try to fix everything. Pick one metric to improve:

| If you want to... | Focus on... |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Hit it farther | Club speed + smash factor |
| Stop slicing | Face angle + path |
| Control distance | Consistent strike location |
| Hit it higher/lower | Launch angle + spin |

### 3. Use the Data

The numbers don't lie. If your spin rate is 4,000 RPM with a 7-iron, you know you need to work on strike quality. If your launch angle is 8°, you might need more loft or a different shaft.

## Finding the Right Venue

Not all simulators are equal. For serious practice, look for:

- Professional-grade launch monitors (TrackMan, Foresight, etc.)
- Adequate space (10ft+ ceilings, 15ft+ depth)
- Coaching availability
- Video analysis options

> "The golfer who uses winter wisely is the one who surprises everyone in April."

## Make a Plan

Instead of random sessions, commit to:

1. **One focused practice per week** (technique work)
2. **One fun session per week** (play Pebble Beach, compete with friends)
3. **Monthly check-ins** to track your baseline numbers
