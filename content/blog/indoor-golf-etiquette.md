Walking into a golf simulator venue for the first time? You're in for a treat. But simulator golf has its own unwritten rules that differ from both the course and the driving range.

## Time Management

Most venues book simulators by the hour. A round of 18 holes typically takes 45-60 minutes per player. If you're playing with a group:

- Be ready to hit when it's your turn
- Use the shot clock if available
- Don't spend 10 minutes looking for the "perfect" tee shot

## Tee It Up (Or Don't)

Unlike a driving range, you're hitting into a screen. You don't need to tee up every shot — in fact, many simulators work better with the ball directly on the mat. Check with the venue's preference or ask the staff.

## Divot Repair

You won't be fixing ball marks on the green, but you **should** fix your divots on the mat. Most venues provide:

- Rubber tees for tee shots
- Divot repair tools
- Brushes for cleaning the hitting surface

Use them. The next group will thank you.

## Food and Drink

Most simulator venues allow food and drinks in the bay — it's part of the experience. Just keep it:

- Away from the equipment
- Off the hitting mat
- Cleaned up when you leave

## Volume Control

Simulator bays are close together. Celebrate that hole-in-one, but remember that the bay next to you might be taking a serious lesson. Keep the energy high, but the volume reasonable.
